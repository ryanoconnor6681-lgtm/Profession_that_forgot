# The Profession That Forgot Its Worth

An interactive piece about why architecture, as a profession, is economically broken — and why the answer is older and stranger than most people realize.

Live: https://ryanoconnor6681-lgtm.github.io/Profession_that_forgot/

## What it is

Eight tabs of long-form analysis, charts, comparisons, and a closing essay. The argument moves from the present-day floor (a 20-year licensed architect makes about $150,000, which puts them roughly where lawyers, doctors, and software engineers were on day one of their careers), through the 1972 antitrust consent decree that broke the AIA's fee schedule, through the 13-year licensure pipeline, into a value-leak analysis comparing architecture to software, music, film, and Sears Roebuck mail-order kit homes, and out the other end into a closing essay tracing the whole thing back to a 1671 French royal academy founded by Louis XIV's finance minister.

The short version: architecture is the only major creative profession with no back-end. No royalty mechanism, no equity, no licensing of accumulated work. The check is cashed at completion. The asset compounds for thirty years for everyone else.

The longer version: the business model architecture is stuck inside is a 355-year-old French aristocratic guild model, transplanted into 1850s America by Richard Morris Hunt, codified by the AIA in 1908, frozen in place by the 1972 consent decree, and still running the profession in 2026. The cultural template (the bespoke commission, the patron-architect relationship, the contempt for stock plans, the gentleman's profession) is older than the United States.

## Tabs

1. The Floor — what architects actually make
2. 1972 — the consent decree, the architect-vs-everyone-else real wage chart, the housing affordability gap
3. The Pipeline — 13.3 years from start of college to license, 37% drop-out
4. The Roark Tax — the cultural mythology, pop-culture vs reality, "everyone is an architect"
5. The Value Leak — eleven sub-sections walking the leak from every angle: slice at completion, appreciation over time, bespoke vs scale, Sears, margin gradient, missing back-end, unsold accumulated IP, the $100M-song thought experiment
6. What If — counterfactual: what 1955 forward looks like without 1972
7. Same Disease, Different Costume — the pattern in adjacent creative fields
8. The Inheritance — the 1671-to-2026 chain, the cultural code that came with it, the closing argument

## Built with

Single-file React via CDN (Babel-in-the-browser, no build step). Tailwind utility classes. Custom serif/sans pairing (Fraunces + Inter). All charts are raw inline SVG. The whole thing is ~2,300 lines in one HTML file, which means you can drop it on any static host and it just works.

## Running locally

Open `index.html` in a browser. That's it. There's no build, no node_modules, no package.json, no toolchain. The piece was deliberately built single-file because the argument should be portable and the artifact should outlive any framework version.

## A note on this piece

I trained as an architect. I went through the early years of IDP, the Master of Architecture program, the studio culture, the all-nighters, the assumption that the work would eventually pay off. I left before licensure to go into experiential marketing, where I now run a creative team at RedPeg. Most of the people I went to school with stayed in the profession. They are excellent at what they do, working harder than almost anyone I know in any other field, and earning compensation that does not reflect that. This piece is for them. It is also for the prospective student about to sign up for a 13-year pipeline who deserves to see the math.

The argument is not that architects are blameless or that the system is anyone's individual fault. The argument is that the model is older than anyone alive, that nobody currently inside the profession picked it, and that the first step out is naming where it came from.
